### Detail — Gradual Seepage (Clause 4.2.1)

Clause 4.2.1: Water damage arising from gradual seepage over a period exceeding 14 days is excluded from coverage.

This is one of the most commonly misunderstood exclusions. It does **not** mean that a leak
must have been known or visible for 14 days; it means the water damage arose from a process
that was ongoing for more than 14 days, even if undetected. Key indicators that an event will
be assessed as gradual seepage:

- Staining or discolouration of walls, ceilings, or floors consistent with prolonged exposure
- Mould or mildew growth (typically requires > 72 hours of moisture, suggesting the event
  was not sudden)
- Multiple wet-dry cycles evidenced by tide marks or layered staining
- Plumber's assessment stating the leak is consistent with a slow, long-running failure
  rather than a sudden burst

Conversely, a sudden pipe burst or appliance overflow that causes water to pool and seep into
walls within a 24-hour period is covered under §3.1, even if the policyholder did not
discover it for several days.

If assessors dispute the characterisation, Meridian will apply the benefit of the doubt to
events that are genuinely ambiguous, consistent with our duty of utmost good faith.

### Detail — Unlisted Jewellery (§7.2 and §7.3.2)

Jewellery items valued above $5,000 must be individually listed on the policy schedule.

This requirement applies per item, not per claim. A diamond engagement ring valued at $8,000
must be individually listed; failure to list it means the claim will be settled at the general
per-item limit of $2,500, regardless of the actual value.

To list a high-value item:
1. Obtain a written appraisal from a qualified jeweller (dated within 5 years).
2. Contact Meridian or your broker to add the item as a "specified item" on the schedule.
3. A small additional premium applies (typically 0.5–1% of the item's insured value per year).

## Section 6: Property Risk Exclusions

| # | Exclusion | Applies when |
|---|-----------|-------------|
| 6.1 | All perils except fire and lightning | Property vacant for > 60 consecutive days |
| 6.2 | All perils except fire | Property under active demolition order |
| 6.3 | Flood | Coastal property without flood risk assessment |

For properties within 100 metres of a coastline or tidal watercourse, the flood extension
cannot be added without a flood risk assessment (per underwriting guidelines). This means
flood events at such properties are excluded unless the assessment has been provided and
the extension added.
