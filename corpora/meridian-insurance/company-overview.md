## Claims Philosophy

Meridian's claims philosophy is built on three principles:

1. **Speed:** We aim to acknowledge all claims within 2 business days and make coverage
   decisions within 10 business days of receiving complete documentation.
2. **Fairness:** We apply the policy terms consistently and in good faith. Where there is
   genuine ambiguity, we apply the benefit of the doubt to the policyholder.
3. **Clarity:** We explain coverage decisions in plain English. If a claim is declined, we
   state the specific exclusion that applies.

## Policy Principles

### Duty of Disclosure

Policyholders have a duty to disclose all facts that a reasonable insurer would consider
material when deciding whether to insure and on what terms. This duty applies at inception,
renewal, and when making any material change to the policy.

Meridian's underwriting team will ask all relevant questions on the application or renewal
form. You are also required to volunteer information that you know is material, even if not
asked.

### Utmost Good Faith

Insurance contracts are based on the principle of utmost good faith. Both parties — Meridian
and the policyholder — must act honestly and disclose material information. Meridian commits
to applying this principle in claims assessment: where evidence is genuinely ambiguous, we
will not interpret it adversarially.
