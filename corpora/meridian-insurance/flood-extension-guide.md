# HomeShield Plus: Flood Extension Guide

**Document:** MI-Flood-Ext-Rev3
**Issuer:** Meridian Insurance Company Limited
**Revision date:** April 2026

---

## About the Flood Extension

The standard HomeShield Plus policy does not cover flood damage (defined as damage caused
by rising water from a natural watercourse, sea, or storm surge). The Flood Extension
adds this cover for an additional premium.

---

## Who Needs the Flood Extension?

Any policyholder whose property could be at risk of flooding from:

- Rising sea levels or storm surge (coastal properties)
- River or creek flooding (riparian properties)
- Stormwater overflow into low-lying areas

**Even if you have not flooded before**, your property may be in a modelled flood zone.
Check the National Flood Risk Map at flood.gov.au (or your local equivalent) before deciding
whether to purchase the extension.

---

## Eligibility Requirements

### Standard Properties

Most properties can add the flood extension without additional requirements. A standard
premium loading applies based on the postcode flood risk rating.

### Coastal and Riparian Properties

Properties located within 100 metres of a coastline require a flood risk assessment before underwriting.

This requirement exists because proximity to the coast significantly increases flood risk from
storm surge and sea-level rise events that are not fully captured by postcode-level risk ratings.

**What the flood risk assessment involves:**

1. A qualified hydrologist or Meridian-approved flood risk surveyor visits the property
   (or completes a desktop assessment using LIDAR and survey data).
2. The assessor compares the property's finished floor level against modelled flood levels
   for 1-in-20, 1-in-100, and 1-in-500 year events.
3. A written report is provided to both the policyholder and Meridian.

**Assessment outcomes:**

| Risk category | Outcome |
|--------------|---------|
| Floor level above 1-in-100 year level | Standard flood extension available |
| Floor level below 1-in-100 year level | Extension available with $5,000 flood excess |
| Floor level below 1-in-20 year level | Extension not available; refer to senior underwriter |

**Note:** The flood risk assessment requirement applies to any property within 100 metres
of a tidal watercourse (ocean, estuary, inlet, river tidal reach) or any non-tidal
watercourse where the modelled flood level exceeds finished floor level.

### Exemptions

The flood risk assessment is not required if the property owner provides:

- A current (< 3 years) flood risk certificate from a qualified hydrologist confirming the
  property is above the 1-in-100-year modelled flood level, **and**
- Written confirmation from the local council that the property is not in a designated
  flood overlay zone

## What the Flood Extension Covers

When the Flood Extension is active:

- Rising water damage from any natural watercourse, sea, estuary, or storm drain
- Contents damaged by flood water entering the property
- Temporary accommodation costs while the property is uninhabitable due to flood damage
- Professional water extraction and drying services (included in settlement)

**Flood extension excess:** The standard accidental damage excess of $500 applies unless:
- A specific flood excess is noted on your policy schedule (may apply for high-risk properties)
- The flood is an earthquake-triggered tsunami (earthquake excess of $2,500 applies)

---

## How to Add the Flood Extension

1. **Check eligibility:** If your property is within 100 metres of a coastline or tidal
   watercourse, arrange a flood risk assessment (see above).
2. **Contact Meridian:** Call 1-800-MER-SVC or log in to your portal to request the extension.
3. **Provide documentation:** If required, upload your flood risk assessment report via the
   portal.
4. **Premium quoted:** Meridian will provide a premium quote. The extension takes effect from
   the date payment is confirmed.

---

## Claims Under the Flood Extension

Flood claims follow the same process as standard claims (see the Claims Process FAQ). Additional
documentation that may be required:

- Flood event records from the Bureau of Meteorology or local council
- Photographs showing flood water levels (e.g., tide marks on walls)
- Professional water damage assessment report

For major flood events, Meridian deploys a rapid-response claims team to affected areas.
Contact 1-800-MER-CLMS for emergency assessment and temporary accommodation assistance.
