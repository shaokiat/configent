# Underwriting Guidelines: HomeShield Plus Personal Lines

**Document:** MI-UW-GL-Rev4
**Issuer:** Meridian Insurance — Underwriting Division
**Revision date:** April 2026
**Audience:** Meridian underwriters and authorised broker partners

*These guidelines are for underwriter and broker use. They are not to be shared with
policyholders. For policyholder-facing information, see the product disclosure statement.*

---

## 1. Risk Appetite

Meridian writes HomeShield Plus for owner-occupied and landlord residential properties with
the following general risk appetite:

- **Target market:** Owner-occupied homes, single-family dwellings and apartments, landlord
  properties with up to 4 units
- **Preferred risk profile:** Property built after 1980, no claims in past 3 years, not
  located in a high-risk flood or wildfire zone
- **Sub-standard risks:** Considered case-by-case with loading or special conditions applied

---

## 2. Property Acceptance Criteria

### 2.1 Construction

| Construction type | Acceptance |
|------------------|-----------|
| Brick, concrete, or masonry | Standard |
| Timber frame with brick veneer | Standard |
| Full timber/weatherboard | Loadable (10–20%) |
| Steel frame | Standard |
| Kit/modular (post-2010) | Standard |
| Unconventional (straw bale, earthen) | Refer to underwriter |

### 2.2 Age

Properties over 40 years old require a building inspection report not older than 5 years.
The report must confirm: roof in good condition, no rising damp, no major structural defects,
and electrical wiring not original (pre-1980 aluminium or knob-and-tube wiring is not accepted
without re-wiring certification).

### 2.3 Occupancy

- Owner-occupied: standard
- Tenanted: +5% loading, short-term holiday let: +15% loading, vacant (> 30 days): refer
  to underwriter
- Commercial use of any part of the property: refer to underwriter

## 3. Geographic Risk Factors

### 3.1 Flood Risk

Properties located within 100 metres of a coastline require a flood risk assessment before underwriting.

This assessment must be:
- Conducted by a qualified hydrologist or a Meridian-approved flood risk surveyor
- Completed within the past 3 years (or re-assessed after any significant storm event)
- Accompanied by a floor level vs. modelled flood level comparison for 1-in-100-year events

Properties within 100 metres of any tidal watercourse (river, estuary, inlet, or ocean) are
subject to the same requirement. Properties within 100 metres of a non-tidal watercourse
(creek, drainage channel) where the 1-in-100-year modelled flood level is above the finished
floor level are also subject to this requirement.

If the flood risk assessment indicates the property is at significant risk (defined as floor
level below the 1-in-20-year flood level), the property may only be written with the flood
extension excluded, or with a flood deductible of $5,000 applied. Refer to the senior
underwriter for properties at extreme risk.

### 3.2 Bushfire / Wildfire Risk

Properties in zones classified as Bushfire Attack Level (BAL) 29 or above require:
- Evidence of construction to BAL-29 standard or above
- A $1,000 bushfire excess applied to any fire event claim

Properties at BAL-FZ (flame zone) are outside risk appetite and not to be written without
CUO approval.

### 3.3 Coastal Erosion

Properties within 50 metres of a cliffedge or actively eroding shoreline are outside risk
appetite for buildings cover. Contents cover only may be considered at CUO's discretion.

### 3.4 Subsidence Risk

Properties in known subsidence zones (mining, expansive clay soils) require a geotechnical
report confirming the foundation is stable.

## 4. Claims History

| History | Treatment |
|---------|-----------|
| No claims in 5 years | No-claims discount applies (5–10%) |
| 1 claim in 3 years | Standard rates |
| 2 or more claims in 3 years | +15–25% loading or refer |
| Single claim > $25,000 in past 2 years | Refer to underwriter |
| Fraud or non-disclosure on prior policy | Decline |

---

## 5. Sum Insured Adequacy

### 5.1 Buildings

Meridian uses replacement cost (not market value) for buildings sum insured. The sum insured
must cover full demolition and rebuild costs, including architect and engineering fees
(typically 10–15% of rebuild cost). Under-insurance provisions apply if sum insured is more
than 20% below the Meridian rebuild estimate.

### 5.2 Contents

For high-value items:
- Individual items over $2,500 must be listed as specified items
- Jewellery over $5,000 per item: must be listed individually on the policy schedule (per
  product wording Section 7)
- Total contents sum insured should reflect full replacement cost of contents at current
  retail prices

---

## 6. Special Conditions Available

Underwriters may apply special conditions where appropriate:

| Condition | Trigger |
|-----------|---------|
| Flood cover excluded | High flood risk without mitigation |
| Security condition: alarm required within 30 days | High-crime area, premium property |
| Inspection condition: roof repair within 90 days | Deferred maintenance found in inspection |
| Bushfire excess $1,000 | BAL 29+ property |
| No water damage cover for listed plumbing | Repeated water damage claims |

---

## 7. Referral and Decline Criteria

**Mandatory refer to senior underwriter:**
- Sum insured > $3,000,000 (buildings + contents)
- Property at BAL-FZ
- More than 2 claims in 3 years
- Vacant > 30 days
- Commercial use of any part

**Mandatory decline (no exceptions):**
- Active fraudulent claims history
- Known demolition order
- Property used for drug manufacturing or other illegal activities
