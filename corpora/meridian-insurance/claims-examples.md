# HomeShield Plus: Claims Examples and Case Studies

**Document:** MI-Claims-Examples-Rev3
**Issuer:** Meridian Insurance — Claims Training and Communications
**Revision date:** January 2026

*These are illustrative examples to help policyholders understand how common claims are
assessed. They do not constitute legal advice or a guarantee of coverage in any specific case.*

---

## Water Damage Examples

### Example 1: Sudden burst pipe (Covered)

**Situation:** A policyholder returns home from work to find water flooding the kitchen.
A hot water pipe under the sink has burst. Damage to cabinets, flooring, and appliances.

**Assessment:** The burst pipe is a sudden, unforeseen event. Damage occurred within hours
of the event. This is covered under §3.1 of the HomeShield Plus policy wording.

**Settlement:** Repair costs of $8,400 settled as follows: claim $8,400, less standard excess
$500 = **$7,900 paid by Meridian**.

**Note:** The excess is $500 regardless of the scope of damage, because this is a standard
accidental damage claim under HomeShield Plus.

---

### Example 2: Gradual ceiling leak (Not covered)

**Situation:** A policyholder notices extensive water staining on the ceiling and walls.
A plumber's report states that roof tiles have been broken and water has been ingressing
for "several months." Mould is present throughout the affected room.

**Assessment:** The damage arose from gradual seepage over a period well exceeding 14 days.
Clause 4.2.1 of the policy wording excludes water damage arising from gradual seepage
over a period exceeding 14 days.

**Settlement:** Claim declined. Meridian recommends the policyholder address the roof
maintenance to prevent further damage.

**Note:** Had the roof tiles been damaged by a sudden storm event, and the policyholder had
reported promptly after the storm, the water ingress following that storm event might have been
covered. The key question is whether there was a sudden, identifiable triggering event.

---

### Example 3: Storm damage leading to water ingress (Covered)

**Situation:** A severe storm causes tiles to crack. Water enters through the cracked tiles
over the following 48 hours, causing ceiling damage. The policyholder reports the claim within
5 days of the storm.

**Assessment:** The triggering event was sudden (the storm). The 48-hour period of water
ingress following the storm is considered consequential damage from a covered event, not
gradual seepage. Covered under §2.2 (windstorm) and §3.1.

**Settlement:** Repair and redecoration costs settled at $3,900, less excess $500 = **$3,400**.

## Jewellery and Valuables Examples

### Example 4: Listed engagement ring stolen (Covered at insured value)

**Situation:** A policyholder's engagement ring (insured value: $12,000, individually listed
on the policy schedule) is stolen in a home burglary.

**Assessment:** The ring is individually listed as a specified item. It is covered at the
listed value of $12,000, subject to proof of ownership and police report.

**Settlement:** $12,000 (agreed value), less theft excess $750 = **$11,250 paid by Meridian**.

---

### Example 5: Unlisted jewellery stolen (Covered only to per-item limit)

**Situation:** A policyholder claims for a bracelet valued at $7,500. It was not individually
listed on the policy schedule.

**Assessment:** Jewellery items valued above $5,000 must be individually listed on the policy
schedule. As this item was not listed, the general per-item contents limit of $2,500 applies.

**Settlement:** $2,500 (per-item limit), less theft excess $750 = **$1,750 paid by Meridian**.
The policyholder is reminded to arrange listing of any other high-value items.

## Liability Examples

### Example 6: Visitor injured on steps (Covered)

**Situation:** A guest slips on icy front steps and breaks their wrist. They sue the
policyholder for $35,000 in medical costs and lost income.

**Assessment:** Accidental bodily injury to a third party at the insured property. Covered
under the liability section up to $2,000,000.

**Settlement:** Meridian handles the legal defence and pays the $35,000 liability claim on
the policyholder's behalf.

---

### Example 7: Home-based business claim (Not covered)

**Situation:** A client visiting the policyholder's home-based consultancy business falls
and claims against the policyholder.

**Assessment:** The liability section excludes liability arising from business activities at
the property (§5.2). The home office extension was not purchased.

**Settlement:** Claim declined for liability arising from business activities. The policyholder
should seek a business liability policy or purchase the home office extension.

---

## Lodgement Deadline Examples

### Example 8: Late claim — exceptional circumstances (Accepted)

**Situation:** A policyholder suffers a serious injury in the same event that damaged their
property and is hospitalised for 6 weeks. They lodge the claim 45 days after the event.

**Assessment:** Claims must be lodged within 30 days of the insured event unless exceptional
circumstances apply. Hospitalisation is a recognised exceptional circumstance. The claim is
accepted with a 15-day late lodgement.

---

### Example 9: Late claim — no exceptional circumstances (Reduced)

**Situation:** A policyholder discovers water damage and lodges a claim 60 days later, citing
a busy work schedule.

**Assessment:** A busy work schedule does not constitute exceptional circumstances. The claim
is processed but subject to a late-lodgement reduction of 10% to reflect Meridian's reduced
ability to assess the fresh evidence.
