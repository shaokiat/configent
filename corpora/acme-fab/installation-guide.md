# PX-900 Installation and Site Requirements Guide

**Document ID:** PX900-IG-Rev3
**Revision Date:** November 2025

---

## 1. Site Requirements

### 1.1 Cleanroom Classification

The PX-900 is designed for installation in Class 10 (ISO 4) or cleaner cleanrooms. The
equipment footprint is 1.8 m × 2.4 m (tool body) + 0.6 m service clearance on three sides.

### 1.2 Utilities

| Utility | Requirement |
|---------|------------|
| Electrical | 208 VAC 3-phase, 60 A, 60 Hz (US); 400 VAC 3-phase, 32 A, 50 Hz (EU) |
| Facility N₂ | 99.999% purity, 80 psig, minimum 20 slpm supply capacity |
| Cooling water | 15–22°C, 15 L/min, < 5 ppm dissolved solids |
| Exhaust (process) | 150 cfm corrosive exhaust to abatement |
| Exhaust (general) | 50 cfm general exhaust |
| Compressed dry air | 60–90 psig, dew point < −40°C |

### 1.3 Floor Loading

The PX-900 installed weight is 1,850 kg. The floor must support a distributed load of
1,200 kg/m². Verify with your facilities engineer before installation.

---

## 2. Delivery and Unpacking

1. Acme ships the PX-900 in a nitrogen-purged crate. Do not break the crate seal until
   the crate is in its final cleanroom position.
2. The crate requires a forklift rated for ≥ 2,200 kg; fork pocket dimensions are
   200 mm × 80 mm.
3. Before breaking the crate seal, verify the humidity indicator card inside the window
   is blue (< 30% RH). If the card is pink, contact Acme Field Service before proceeding.

---

## 3. Installation Sequence

1. **Site survey** — Acme applications engineer visits ≥ 2 weeks before delivery to
   confirm utility readiness and cleanroom layout.
2. **Delivery and positioning** — Tool placed on levelling feet; level to < 0.1 mm/m.
3. **Utility connections** — Acme field engineer connects all utilities; customer
   facilities team provides and connects the facility side.
4. **Qualification (IQ/OQ)** — 3-day installation and operational qualification:
   - IQ: confirm all hardware installed per spec
   - OQ: verify tool meets performance spec with qualification wafers
5. **Process qualification (PQ)** — Optional; Acme Process Engineering can assist.
6. **Operator training** — 2-day onsite training for up to 4 operators (included with
   purchase; additional sessions at T&M rate).

---

## 4. First-Time Startup

1. Power on main breaker.
2. Wait for all subsystems to initialise (approximately 15 minutes).
3. Pump down to base pressure (target: < 5 × 10⁻⁷ Torr; may take 4–8 hours on first startup
   due to outgassing from new components).
4. Run bake-out recipe BAKE-01 (120°C chamber wall, 12 hours) to accelerate outgassing.
5. After bake-out, verify base pressure and run the dry-clean recipe C-01 before processing.
